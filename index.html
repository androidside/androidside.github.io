About

I am a curious engineer that loves to travel and meet new people.

I currently work at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, near Washington DC. Our project is the Balloon Experimental Twin Telescope for Infrared Interferometry. Like all the NASA missions, we also have short and cool name: BETTII.

Over the last 2 years I have been involved in many areas of the BETTII project. My tasks consist in optimizing the processes that run on the FPGA; I am in charge of the control system that adjusts the pointing and attitude control mechanisms in space. I have also developed software for the Telemetry system, GPS, star-detectors, temperature or power monitoring systems.

On the side I have been developing the full software infrastructure for a drone quadcopter (Phoenix) running on the atmega328p microcontroller, I aim to further develop Phoenix adding obstacle avoidance systems and self-flight control capabilities.

During my studies in France at the Aeronautical University ISAE-Supaero I was the Systems Engineering lead in a project that aimed to simulate an optical communications link between a ground station and a geostationary satellite. A similar position was held by me for an image processing project aimed to detect the outline of nearby objects for unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV/Drones) in order to position themselves in the space.

During summers I have been working as safari and horse-trails guide in South Africa, Botswana, Iceland, Tunisia or Ireland.

Resume

Countless are the challenges awaiting space-based technologies: improve weather forecasting, ground and air navigation, telecommunications or disaster monitoring systems. Beyond Earth and its LEO environment we have the challenge of reaching Mars, improve our knowledge about the other planets of the Solar System but also study Star formation and search for exoplanets and forms of life. I studied Engineering of Telecommunications (UPC–Barcelona, Spain), a Bachelor plus a Master program based on a curriculum of fundamental sciences that instructed me on a methodology to tackle problems effectively, I focused my studies on Signal Processing and Communication Networks. I completed my education at the Aeronautical Engineering School ISAE-Supaero (France,Toulouse), where I specialized in Embedded Systems in Airplanes, Satellite Communications and Navigation.
