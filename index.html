About

I am a curious engineer that loves to travel and meet new people.

I currently work at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, near Washington D.C. Our project is the Balloon Experimental Twin Telescope for Infrared Interferometry. Like all the NASA missions, we also have a short and cool name: BETTII.

Over the last 2 years I have been involved in many areas of the BETTII project. My tasks consist of optimizing the processes that run on the field programmable gate array (FPGA); I am responsible for the control system that adjusts the pointing and attitude control mechanisms in space. I have also developed software for the telemetry system, GPS, star-detectors, temperature and power monitoring systems.

On the side, I have been developing the full software infrastructure for a drone quadcopter (Phoenix) running on the atmega2560 microcontroller. I aim to further develop Phoenix adding obstacle avoidance systems and self-flight control capabilities.

During my studies in France, at the Aeronautical University ISAE-Supaero, I was the Systems Engineering lead in a project that aimed to simulate an optical communications link between a ground station and a geostationary satellite. A similar position was held by me for an image processing project aimed to detect the outline of nearby objects for unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV/Drones) in order to position themselves in the space.

During the summers of my studies I was working as a safari and horse-trails guide in countries such as South Africa, Botswana, Iceland, Tunisia and Ireland. I did equestrian reports published in various specialized horse magazines : Ecuestre and El Mundo del Caballo
